About Frankston Farm

Richard and Lynn Frankston were born in regional WA, most precisely in Westville. As most regional town folk do, Richard and Lynn worked in farms in the area over their teenage summers and crop times.

For both Lynn and Richard, what started as work, soon became a passion and vocation. They set out to start their own farm in 1985. The Frankston Farm was born.

From the humble wheat crop beginnings, Frankston Farm became a powerhouse in the agricultural sector of Westville and South Western WA.

More recently, the Farm took an approach to extend their arms into the Westville community. Frankston Farm has now opened their gates to the public to come and know the farm, production, sustainability initiatives and their lovely animals.

The farm has horses, cattle, donkeys, sheep, goats, alpacas, rabbits, guinea pigs, chickens, ducks and pigs.
